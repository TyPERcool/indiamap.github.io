Haryana

Haryana is a state located in northern India. It is bordered by Punjab and Himachal Pradesh to the north, Uttar Pradesh to the east, and Rajasthan to the west and south. Haryana is known for its agricultural productivity and its proximity to the national capital, New Delhi. The state’s capital is Chandigarh, which is a shared capital with Punjab and is renowned for its modern architecture and urban planning.

   Haryana has a rich historical and cultural heritage, with significant historical sites such as Kurukshetra, the epicenter of the Mahabharata battle. The state is also known for its vibrant cultural traditions, including folk music and dance forms such as the Gidda and Bhangra.

   The state's economy is primarily driven by agriculture, with significant contributions from sectors like manufacturing and services. Haryana is also a hub for education and research, with numerous educational institutions and research centers located in the state.



Districts in Haryana

Haryana is divided into 22 districts. Each district has its own unique features and contributes to the state’s overall development.
