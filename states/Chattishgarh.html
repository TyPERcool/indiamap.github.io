Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is a state located in central India. It was formed on November 1, 2000, by separating 16 Chhattisgarhi-speaking districts from Madhya Pradesh. The state is bordered by Madhya Pradesh to the northwest, Maharashtra to the west, Telangana to the southwest, Andhra Pradesh to the south, Odisha to the east, and Jharkhand to the northeast. Chhattisgarh is known for its rich mineral resources, dense forests, and vibrant tribal culture. The state's capital is Raipur, which is also the largest city in the state. Chhattisgarh is an important hub for steel and power industries and has significant agricultural production, particularly rice.

   The state has a diverse cultural heritage, with many tribal communities contributing to its unique traditions and festivals. Chhattisgarh is home to several wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, including the Kanger Valley National Park and the Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary. The state's economy is driven by industries such as mining, steel production, and agriculture, alongside a growing tourism sector.

   Chhattisgarh also hosts numerous ancient temples and historical sites, which attract tourists and history enthusiasts alike. The state celebrates various festivals with grandeur, reflecting its rich cultural tapestry.



Districts in Chhattisgarh

As of 2021, Chhattisgarh is divided into 33 districts. The state has undergone several changes in its administrative divisions since its formation, with new districts being created to improve governance and development.
