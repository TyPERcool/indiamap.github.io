Telangana

Telangana is a state located in the southern part of India. It was formed in 2014 when it was separated from the northwestern part of Andhra Pradesh. The state is bordered by Maharashtra to the northwest, Chhattisgarh to the north, Andhra Pradesh to the east, and Karnataka to the west. The capital of Telangana is Hyderabad, which is also the de jure capital of Andhra Pradesh.

   Telangana is known for its rich cultural heritage, historic monuments, and diverse cuisine. Hyderabad, the capital city, is famous for its historical landmarks such as the Charminar and the Golconda Fort, as well as its vibrant food culture, including the renowned Hyderabadi Biryani.

   The state’s economy is diverse, with significant contributions from information technology, pharmaceuticals, and manufacturing. Agriculture also plays a crucial role, with major crops including rice, maize, and cotton. Telangana's unique blend of historical significance, economic vitality, and cultural heritage makes it a prominent state in southern India.



Districts in Telangana

Telangana is divided into 33 districts. Each district has its own unique features and contributes to the state’s overall diversity.
