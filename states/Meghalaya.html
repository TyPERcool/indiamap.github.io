Meghalaya

Meghalaya is a state located in the northeastern part of India. It is bordered by Assam to the north and east, and Bangladesh to the south and west. Known for its picturesque landscapes, including lush green hills and beautiful waterfalls, Meghalaya is often referred to as the "Abode of Clouds." The state’s capital is Shillong, which is renowned for its scenic beauty and pleasant climate.

   Meghalaya is characterized by its high rainfall, which supports its dense forests and rich biodiversity. The state is home to unique tribes such as the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garos, each with its own distinct culture and traditions. Meghalaya is famous for its living root bridges, which are woven from the roots of rubber trees and are a marvel of traditional engineering.

   The state’s economy is primarily based on agriculture, with rice and maize being the major crops. Tourism also plays a significant role, with visitors attracted to its natural beauty and cultural heritage. Meghalaya's vibrant festivals, such as Wangala and Shad Suk Mynsiem, add to its charm and cultural richness.
