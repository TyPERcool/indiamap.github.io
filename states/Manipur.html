Manipur

Manipur is a state located in the northeastern region of India. It is bordered by Nagaland to the north, Assam to the west, and Mizoram to the south. To the east, it shares an international border with Myanmar. The stateâ€™s capital is Imphal, which is known for its scenic beauty and cultural heritage.

   Manipur is renowned for its lush landscapes, including rolling hills, valleys, and beautiful lakes. The state is often called the "Jewel of the East" due to its rich cultural traditions and natural beauty. Manipur is known for its classical dance forms, particularly Manipuri dance, and its vibrant festivals such as Yaoshang (Holi) and Ningol Chakouba.

   The state's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with rice being the staple crop. Handloom and handicrafts are also significant contributors to the local economy. Manipur's unique cultural heritage and natural resources make it an important state in India's northeastern region.
