Jharkhand

Jharkhand is a state located in eastern India. It was carved out of the southern part of Bihar on November 15, 2000. Jharkhand is bordered by Bihar to the north, West Bengal to the east, Odisha to the south, and Chhattisgarh to the west. Known for its rich mineral resources and dense forests, Jharkhand is an important center for mining and industry. The state’s capital is Ranchi, which is also one of the major cities and a key economic hub.

   Jharkhand has a diverse cultural heritage with numerous tribal communities contributing to its unique traditions. The state is known for its vibrant festivals, traditional dance forms, and handicrafts. It is also home to several natural attractions, including waterfalls, national parks, and wildlife sanctuaries.

   The state’s economy is driven by mining, with significant deposits of coal, iron ore, and other minerals. Additionally, agriculture and manufacturing sectors play important roles in Jharkhand’s economic landscape. The state’s rich natural resources and cultural diversity make it a unique and significant region in India.

Jharkhand is divided into 24 districts. These districts are known for their varied geographical features and cultural diversity.
