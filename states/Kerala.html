Kerala

Kerala is a state located on the southwestern coast of India. Known for its serene backwaters, lush green landscapes, and rich cultural heritage, Kerala is often referred to as "God's Own Country." The state is bordered by Karnataka to the north, Tamil Nadu to the east, and the Arabian Sea to the west. The capital of Kerala is Thiruvananthapuram, a city known for its historical monuments and vibrant cultural scene.

   Kerala is famous for its unique geography, including its network of backwaters, which are a popular attraction for tourists. The state's landscape also features beautiful beaches, hill stations, and lush forests. Kerala is renowned for its traditional arts, including Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and its vibrant festivals like Onam.

   The economy of Kerala is driven by agriculture, tourism, and remittances from the Keralite diaspora. The state's emphasis on education and healthcare has also contributed to its high human development indicators. Kerala's rich tradition of social reform and progressive policies has made it a model for development in India.

Kerala is divided into 14 districts. Each district has its own unique attractions and contributes to the state's overall charm and cultural diversity.
