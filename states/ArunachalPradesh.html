Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is a state in the northeastern part of India. It is the largest of the Seven Sister States of Northeast India by area. Arunachal Pradesh borders the states of Assam and Nagaland to the south, and shares international borders with Bhutan to the west, Myanmar to the east, and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north. Itanagar is the state capital, and the state is known for its diverse culture, rich tribal heritage, and stunning natural beauty, including dense forests, rivers, and the Himalayan mountain range.

   The history of Arunachal Pradesh is deeply intertwined with the many indigenous tribes that inhabit the region, each with its own distinct culture, language, and traditions. The state is home to more than 26 major tribes and numerous sub-tribes. The most prominent tribes include the Adi, Nyishi, Apatani, Galo, and Monpa. Traditional festivals such as Losar, Dree, and Nyokum are celebrated with great enthusiasm.

   Arunachal Pradesh is predominantly an agrarian state, with agriculture being the primary source of livelihood. The state is also rich in biodiversity and is known for its various wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, including the Namdapha National Park and the Pakke Tiger Reserve. The state's economy is also supported by tourism, with visitors drawn to its scenic landscapes, historical sites, and vibrant cultural festivals.



Districts in Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh is divided into 25 districts, with each district having its own unique culture and traditions. These districts have been established to ensure efficient administration and to cater to the specific needs of the diverse tribal population.
