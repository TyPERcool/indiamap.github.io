Odisha

Odisha, formerly known as Orissa, is a state located on the eastern coast of India, along the Bay of Bengal. It is bordered by West Bengal to the north, Jharkhand to the northwest, Chhattisgarh to the west, and Andhra Pradesh to the south. The stateâ€™s capital is Bhubaneswar, which is known for its ancient temples and vibrant culture.

   Odisha is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, including classical dance forms like Odissi and its unique festivals such as Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) in Puri. The state is also known for its beautiful beaches, ancient temples, and wildlife sanctuaries. The Konark Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of the most famous landmarks in Odisha.

   The state's economy is primarily based on agriculture, with rice, pulses, and oilseeds being the main crops. Odisha is also rich in mineral resources, and industries such as steel, aluminum, and textiles play a significant role in its economic development. The state's diverse cultural traditions and natural attractions make it an important destination in eastern India.



Districts in Odisha

Odisha is divided into 30 districts. Each district has its own unique geographical and cultural attributes.
